# Phase 1 Visual Teardown — Best-in-Class Navigation (outside WordPress)

**Purpose:** ground-up rebuild reference for the WP navigation block set. Research only — no project code touched.

**Method note:** the MCP-managed Playwright browser was locked by a concurrent Claude Code session for the whole task, so this teardown was captured with an isolated Python-Playwright instance (separate Chromium process, headless, `device_scale_factor` 1.2–1.5). All 4 target sites were reachable — no substitutions were needed, though target 3 used the brief's own "e.g. nike.com" option.

**Sites captured:** Stripe (stripe.com), Apple (apple.com), Nike (nike.com), Shopify (shopify.com). Breakpoints: 1440×900, 768×1024, 375×812.

Every number below was measured via `getBoundingClientRect()` / `getComputedStyle()` on the live page, not eyeballed. Where something could not be confirmed this way, it's flagged explicitly rather than guessed.

- Drawer open 768: `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-stripe-768-drawer-open.png`

**Header height / scroll behaviour:** 76px at 1440, 66px at 768/375. `position: relative`, `z-index: 2` — **NOT sticky**. Confirmed by scrolling 1800px: the header scrolls away with the page entirely and never reappears (screenshot above). This is a deliberate choice on Stripe's very long, heavily-animated homepage, not an oversight — flagged under Design choices, not copied as a default.

**Bar layout (1440):** logo | Products / Solutions / Developers / Resources (each a `<button>` with a chevron-down icon, hover-triggered) | Pricing (plain link, no dropdown) — right-aligned: Sign in (text link) | Contact sales (solid indigo pill button with trailing chevron).

**Mega-menu structure ("Products", hover-triggered):**
- 5 columns: Payments / Revenue / Money Management / Platforms and marketplaces / More. Each link = bold indigo title + grey one-line description underneath, in a tight vertical list (no icons).
- 5th column also carries a **featured content card**: 16:9 video-thumbnail image, title ("Stripe Sessions 2026"), 2-line description, "Watch now ›" link — the mega menu doubles as a marketing surface.
- Panel is **content-width, not full-viewport-bleed** — it's inset to the same left/right margin as the page's hero content (~124px each side at 1440), unlike the other 3 sites.
- Trigger: `aria-expanded="true/false"` on the button; chevron SVG rotates 180°.
- **Backdrop:** `.hds-navigation-menu__overlay { backdrop-filter: blur(5px) }` — dims/blurs the rest of the page while the panel is open. Confirmed via computed style.
- Panel background/shadow/radius: not individually isolated (time-boxed), but screenshot shows a flat white panel with a soft top edge, no visible border-radius.

**Mobile drawer (768 & 375 — identical pattern):** **full-screen takeover**, not a partial-width panel.
- Close = a rounded lavender-chip "✕" button, replaces the hamburger **in place** (top-right).
- Root list: Products / Solutions / Developers / Resources (drill-down rows, trailing "›" chevron) then Pricing / Sign in (plain rows, no chevron). Rows separated by a 1px **dotted** hairline.
- Drill-down confirmed live: tapping "Products" slides to a new screen with "‹ Back" top-left (close ✕ still visible top-right) and a bold section heading + coloured underline rule ("Payments"), then the exact same multi-column desktop content **flattened into one scrollable list** — sub-headings act as simple dividers, not collapsible accordions (zero content dropped between desktop and mobile).
- **Sticky footer CTA pair**, pinned to the very bottom of the drawer in both the root list and the drilled-down screen: "Start now" (solid indigo pill) + "Contact sales" (outline pill).

## 2. Apple (apple.com)

**Screenshots:**
- Resting 1440: `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-apple-1440-resting.png`
- Mac mega open 1440: `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-apple-1440-mac-mega2.png`
- Breakpoint check 1200/1024/900: `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-apple-bp-1200.png`, `-bp-1024.png`, `-bp-900.png`
- Drawer open 375: `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-apple-375-drawer-open.png`
- Drawer open 768 (identical to 375): `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-apple-768-drawer-open.png`

**Header height / scroll behaviour:** exactly **44px at all 3 breakpoints tested** — the slimmest bar of the four sites. `position: fixed`, `z-index: 9999`, background `rgba(255,255,255,0.8)`. Confirmed via scroll tests at 300px, 1200px, and scroll-up from 1200→900: height, position and transform were **identical in every state** — it never hides, never shrinks, never reappears differently. The most persistent/stable header of the four. (Note: a separate dismissible "choose your region" banner sits *above* the nav on first load — that's page content, not nav chrome, and pushes the nav down until closed.)

**Bar layout (1440):** Apple logo (itself a small system-menu trigger) | Store · Mac · iPad · iPhone · Watch · Vision · AirPods · TV & Home · Entertainment · Accessories · Support (11 flat text links, small ~12px tightly-kerned caps, **no chevrons visible at rest**) | search icon | bag icon. No CTA button at all — pure wayfinding + 2 icons.

**Breakpoint collapse point:** full 11-item bar still present at 1024px and 900px; already collapsed to the compact hamburger bar by 768px. Not bisected further — collapse happens somewhere in the ~768–900px range.

**Mega-menu ("Mac", hover-triggered):**
- `div#globalnav-submenu-link-mac`, class `.globalnav-flyout.globalnav-submenu`. Background `rgb(250,250,252)` (near-white, not pure white). **Full viewport width, zero border-radius, zero box-shadow** — sits flush against the 44px bar with no gap.
- Animates via `transition: height 0.254s cubic-bezier(0.4,0,0.6,1)` — the panel's actual pixel HEIGHT is what's animated (measured 552px for the Mac panel), not opacity/transform — so each menu grows to fit its own content exactly.
- Structure: 3 plain-text columns ("Explore Mac" = 7 bold ~24px product-line links, no descriptions, no thumbnails; "Shop Mac" and "More from Mac" = smaller utility links). **Zero imagery anywhere in the panel** — a purely typographic mega menu, the opposite end of the spectrum from Shopify's photo-led one.
- **Backdrop:** `.globalnav-curtain { backdrop-filter: blur(20px) }` — 4× stronger blur than Stripe's, confirmed via computed style.

**Mobile/tablet drawer — 375 and 768 use the IDENTICAL treatment** (not a scaled-down desktop nav, not a hybrid): full-screen (100vw×100vh) overlay, `rgb(250,250,252)` background (matches the desktop flyout colour exactly), plain "✕" icon top-right (no button chip). Single column of the same 10 links, bold ~28px, ~66px row height, generous whitespace. **No chevrons, no drill-down at all** in the mobile menu — every row is a direct link straight to that section (Apple deliberately drops the desktop sub-navigation on mobile rather than nesting it). Toggle button `#globalnav-menutrigger-button`, `aria-label="Menu"`.

- Drawer open 768: `../../reports/visual-diff/teardown-nike-768-drawer-open.png`

**Header height / scroll behaviour — shrink-and-stick:** 96px at rest on desktop = a 36px slim **utility strip** (Find a Store | Help | Join Us | Sign In, right-aligned, light grey background) sitting on top of a 60px **main shop bar**. Both are inside one `position: fixed` element (`z-index: 3`, `transition: transform 0.15s`). On scroll past ~36px the whole nav gets `transform: translateY(-36px)` (class flips to add `topbar-hidden`) — this precisely tucks the utility strip out of view while the 60px shop bar stays fixed and fully visible: **net 96px → 60px shrink, and it stays stuck throughout, never fully hiding.** Caught it mid-reversal scrolling back up (translateY at ‑2.7px after 400ms), confirming it responds to scroll **direction**, not just position — classic hide-utility/keep-shop-nav behaviour. At 768/375 the utility strip is absent even at rest (compact icon bar from the start).

**Bar layout (1440):** Jordan + Converse brand icons (small, left of main logo) | Nike swoosh | New · Men · Women · Kids · Sport · NikeSKIMS · NikeFootball (plain text links, active tab gets an **underline indicator**, not a chevron) | Search field | wishlist heart icon | bag icon. At 768/375: swoosh | search/account/bag icons | hamburger — text links dropped entirely.

**Mega-menu ("Men", hover-triggered):** full-viewport-width white panel, edge-to-edge, no border-radius, no visible box-shadow (not individually confirmed via computed style — visual observation only). 5 plain-text columns: Highlights / Shoes / Clothing / Sport / Brands — bold column headers, regular-weight links below, **no images, no featured card** (the densest, flattest sitemap-style panel of the four). Animation: inner panel `max-height: 0.25s`, outer wrapper `transform 0.1s, opacity 0.1s` — the fastest transition of the four sites.

**Mobile/tablet drawer — 375 and 768 identical:** **slides in from the RIGHT**, not full-screen — the only right-edge drawer of the four sites tested. Measured: width 320px of a 375px viewport (~85%), full 812px height, `position: absolute; right: 0`, `transition: transform 0.25s`, white background. The remaining ~15% sliver of the page stays visible on the left (dimmed page content itself provides the "peek" effect — no separate backdrop-dim layer was detected). Content: New/Men/Women/Kids/Sport/NikeSKIMS (drill-down rows, "›" chevron) + NikeFootball (plain link, no submenu so no chevron) — then a visually separated block of house sub-brand shortcuts (Jordan, Converse, each with its own icon), then a membership promo blurb with an inline "Learn more" link, then a CTA pair (Join Us solid black pill + Sign In outline pill). Close = a circular grey "✕" chip **floating independently above the header row**, not replacing the hamburger in place.

## PATTERNS TO STEAL

Concrete, WP-buildable ideas worth reproducing in the rebuilt nav block set, ranked by how directly they'd lift the framework above a typical WP mega-menu plugin:

1. **Backdrop blur/dim behind an open mega menu** (Stripe `blur(5px)`, Apple `blur(20px)`). Makes the open panel feel focused without a full lightbox. Cheap to build: a sibling overlay `<div>` with `backdrop-filter: blur()`, toggled by the same open-state class as the panel.
2. **Height-animated panel expand, not a fixed max-height guess** (Apple: `transition: height 0.254s cubic-bezier(0.4,0,0.6,1)`; Nike: `max-height 0.25s`). Each menu's panel grows to its own real content height — no clipped content, no empty whitespace for shorter menus.
3. **Context-aware header theming** (Shopify: transparent header, text colour driven by a CSS custom property the section behind it sets). Directly buildable in the WP theme via a per-section `data-nav-theme` attribute + CSS var — avoids a hard scroll-triggered light/dark swap.
4. **Featured/promo slot inside the mega menu** (Stripe's video-thumbnail card; Shopify's 2-tile "Built into every store" sidebar). Turns the nav into a lightweight marketing surface for a client's own promos/events without adding chrome elsewhere on the page.
5. **Shrink the utility strip, not the whole header, on scroll** (Nike: `translateY(-36px)` tucks away the secondary utility row but keeps the primary shop nav permanently reachable and fixed). Better than an all-or-nothing "hide entire header" pattern that briefly removes wayfinding.
6. **Active-state underline instead of a chevron on every item** (Nike's underline on the hovered/active top-level tab; Apple shows almost no chevrons at rest at all). Reads as more premium than chevrons everywhere; reserve the chevron/arrow purely for "this item has a submenu."
7. **Drill-down mobile menu with a persistent Back AND Close, content never dropped** (Stripe: "‹ Back" and "✕" both always visible; the full desktop mega-menu content is flattened into one scrollable list on mobile, not summarised or cut). Sub-headings become plain list dividers rather than collapsible accordions — fewer taps to scan everything.
8. **Full-bleed panels with zero border-radius/shadow** (Apple, Nike, Shopify) so the panel reads as an extension of the header itself, not a "floating card" — Stripe is the one outlier here (its panel is inset to the page's content width).
9. **Non-sticky header as a configurable OPTION, not the default** (Stripe's homepage genuinely has no persistent nav at all). Worth exposing as a real toggle in the WP nav block rather than assuming every client wants a permanently visible bar — but default to sticky, since 3 of the 4 sites here keep the nav reachable at all times.

## MOBILE DRAWER — what the best ones do

- **Two legitimate structural models**, both seen twice: full-screen takeover (Stripe, Apple, Shopify) vs a right-edge slide-in panel that leaves a "peek" sliver of the page visible on the left (Nike). Full-screen is simpler to reason about for accessibility (the focus trap just covers the whole viewport); the slide-in feels lighter/faster but needs its own dim layer and careful focus-trap boundary.
- **Drill-down (Stripe, Nike) vs fully flattened (Apple)** — drill-down preserves the entire desktop mega-menu on mobile without one giant scroll; flattening to direct links only works because Apple's desktop nav is already shallow (no nested sub-menu content beyond the flyout headings). Shopify sits between the two: drill-down arrows exist, but only 2 of its 5 top items actually have one.
- **Close affordance varies in a meaningful way**: it either replaces the hamburger exactly in place (Apple, Shopify — same corner, same size) or floats as an independent chip that doesn't share a position with the hamburger (Stripe's lavender chip, Nike's grey circle sitting above the header row entirely). The floating-independent approach lets the whole header row itself be swapped out or hidden while the drawer is open.
- **Every single site pins its primary conversion CTA(s) to the bottom of the drawer**: Stripe (Start now / Contact sales), Nike (Join Us / Sign In), Shopify (Log in / Start for free). The drawer isn't treated as pure navigation — it's a secondary landing surface for the site's core conversion actions, always reachable without scrolling the link list.
- **All 4 sites collapse to the SAME drawer pattern at 768px as they do at 375px** — none shipped an intermediate "tablet-specific" nav layout; only Apple's full desktop bar survives past 768px at all (down to ~900-1024px), and even Apple's drawer at 768 is pixel-for-pixel the same as its 375 drawer. Strong, consistent signal for the rebuild: design ONE collapsed-nav breakpoint, not a separate tablet treatment.
- **Row dividers are always a subtle 1px hairline** (dotted for Stripe, solid low-opacity white for Shopify) — never a hard border. Keeps a genuinely link-dense drawer (Stripe's drilled-down Payments list ran to 10+ rows) feeling light rather than boxy.

## What could not be confirmed (flagged, not guessed)

- Apple's 44px bar background is `rgba(255,255,255,0.8)`, strongly suggestive of a `backdrop-filter` on the bar itself (to keep it legible over scrolling content) — this specific property was not directly queried on the bar element, only inferred from the alpha value.
- Apple's exact desktop→compact collapse breakpoint was bounded to the 768–900px range, not bisected to an exact pixel.
- Nike's mega-panel box-shadow/border was judged from the screenshot only (appears flush/shadowless); not confirmed via `getComputedStyle`.
- Shopify's mega-panel open/close transition timing was not captured — the follow-up DOM query for it returned empty (element lookup issue, not re-attempted given time budget).
