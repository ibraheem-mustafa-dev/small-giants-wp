## Why this matters

Mama's is the only UK lactation cookie brand positioned to get halal certification. Boobbix, TheMilkBooster, and Milk It Bakehouse have none. Once you have it, they can't claim it without going through the same process.

But there are two different certification bodies with different standards — and one of them may require you to change the recipe.

---

## The two options

### Option A — HFA (Halal Food Authority)
**Recommendation: DO THIS FIRST.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Standard:** Mainstream UK retail acceptance. Most UK supermarkets, Halal Street UK, and major gift platforms accept HFA. |
| **Cost:** ~£200–400/year (annual cert fee). First-year all-in with audit/inspection: ~£500–800. |
| **Timeline:** 4–8 weeks from application. |
| **Recipe change needed:** Likely none. Standard brewer's yeast is generally acceptable under HFA. |
| **Who trusts it:** Most UK Muslim mums, mainstream UK halal shoppers, Halal Street UK, most corporate clients. |
| **Who may not trust it:** Stricter scholars and community members who follow HMC — a minority but vocal in some circles. |

**First action:** Email info@halalfoodauthority.com for a new-applicant information pack — 10 minutes.

---

### Option B — HMC (Halal Monitoring Committee)
**Recommendation: Plan for this 6–12 months after HFA, NOT instead of HFA.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Standard:** Stricter — follows guidance from scholars who consider brewery-derived brewer's yeast impermissible. Widely trusted by the Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and some South Asian Arab communities in the UK. |
| **Cost:** Higher than HFA — exact figures require direct enquiry with HMC. |
| **Timeline:** Longer, and requires recipe approval first. |
| **Recipe change needed:** **YES, almost certainly.** Brewer's yeast sourced from brewery waste is widely considered impermissible under the principles HMC follows. You would need to replace it with an alternative galactagogue — nutritional yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae grown on molasses, not brewery waste) or toasted oat flour or a combination. This affects the product's galactagogue profile — you'd need to test that the replacement works as well. |
| **Who trusts it:** Stricter Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and some Arab Muslim consumers. |

## Why HFA first — not HMC

1. **Speed matters.** Milk It Bakehouse already markets as "halal-friendly" without any cert. They could apply to HFA in the same window you're waiting. Every week of delay narrows your first-mover advantage.

2. **HFA opens most of the market.** The overwhelming majority of UK Muslim shoppers accept HFA. The HMC audience is a subset — important, but worth reaching in month 7, not before you have any cert at all.

3. **Recipe reformulation takes time.** Swapping brewer's yeast for nutritional yeast may change the taste, texture, and galactagogue density. That deserves proper testing, not a rushed decision.

4. **HFA is additive, not compromising.** Getting HFA first and HMC later is a stronger story than waiting for HMC and having no cert for a year.

---

## Before you apply to HFA — one question to resolve

**Does your current brewer's yeast supplier provide halal-suitable brewer's yeast?**

HFA may require you to confirm your brewer's yeast is from a halal-certified source (not brewery-waste derived). This is easy to check with your supplier — one email, 10 minutes.

If yes → apply to HFA immediately.
If no → source a halal-suitable brewer's yeast before applying. Usually straightforward — several UK suppliers offer this.
