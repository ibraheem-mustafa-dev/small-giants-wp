### How I Structure the Middle

**Typical Flow:**
1. Context/situation setup
2. Specific observations about people or experience
3. Reflection on what stood out / what it means
4. Values-based takeaway or personal insight
5. Future intention or invitation

**Key Middle Techniques:**
- Names specific people and what they contributed
- Includes honest self-reflection
- Asks rhetorical questions: "Sound familiar?"
- Uses em dashes for emphasis
- Breaks into short paragraphs (1–3 sentences)

## SECTION 6: Stop Doing This

### Patterns to Avoid

**1. Hashtag Overload**
- Some posts have 6–8 hashtags
- Keep to 3–5 maximum
- Remove generic ones (#BusinessGrowth) if you have specific ones (#MuslimsInConstruction)

**2. Posts That Run Too Long**
- Some posts exceed comfortable reading length
- Consider trimming middle sections
- Every paragraph should add new value

**3. Professional Jargon Moments**
- Occasional phrases like "explosive year-on-year growth" feel less "you"
- Stick to conversational language

**4. Missing the Self-Aware Humour**
- Posts without the playful asides feel flatter
- Don't lose the "(haha)" moments

## SECTION 7: Writing Principles Extracted

### 7 Rules Based on Actual Data

**1. Lead with Gratitude or Emotion, Not Sales**
- 12/18 posts open with thanks, excitement, or personal story
- Hook is relational, not promotional

**2. Celebrate the Community First, Yourself Second**
- Tag specific people and their contributions
- Make others the heroes of the story
- Your role is facilitator/connector/helper

**3. Be Vulnerable About Real Struggles**
- ADHD, learning challenges, analysis paralysis
- Not for sympathy — for connection and normalisation
- Follow vulnerability with insight or growth

**4. Integrate Faith Naturally, Not Performatively**
- "JazakAllah Khair" when genuinely grateful
- "InshaAllah" when discussing future plans
- Islamic values shown through actions, not preached

**5. Use Parenthetical Asides for Personality**
- Self-deprecating humour
- Inside jokes with your audience "(ADHDers will know)"
- Keeps posts human, not polished

**6. Close with Values, Not CTAs**
- Return to principles
- "When the right audience finds the right business, everybody wins"
- Invitations, not sales pitches

**7. Respond to Comments Like a Human**
- Use their name
- Match their energy
- Never templated, always personal
