# nav-menu manifest + resting-state fix — visual verification

## What changed
Manifested under Spec 35 (6 elements: wrapper/bar/item/underline/featured/burger).
Of nav-menu's 9 hover attrs, 8 already had bases from the 2026-07-20 rework. The one
genuine defect: `burgerHoverColour` drove a hover background with NO resting background
attribute — a client could style the burger's hover fill but never its resting fill.
Added `burgerBg` + inspector control.

No style.css change was needed: the resting `background:none` (style.css:76) already
renders byte-identical when burgerBg is unset. The fix is additive — attribute plus
scoped-style emission in render.php.

## LOGGED, NOT FIXED — product limitation
The `item` element's `hover` and `selected` states share an IDENTICAL attrMap because
render.php:304-309 groups `:hover`, `:focus-visible` and `[aria-current="page"]` into one
CSS selector. Consequence: a client CANNOT style the current page differently from a
hovered link. That is honest modelling of the current code, not a manifest bug, but
"you are here" vs "you are pointing at this" is a normal thing to want on a nav.
Splitting that selector changes live navigation styling on every page — bigger blast
radius than this task, deserves its own decision.
